The capital of Japan is Tokyo. Tokyo has been the capital city of Japan since 1868, when it was renamed from Edo. Before Tokyo, Kyoto was the official capital for more than a millennium, and Nagaoka-Kyo was the capital for a brief period in the 8th century. 

The author of Harry Potter is J.K. Rowling, a British author and philanthropist. She wrote the seven-volume children’s fantasy series from 1997 to 2007, and it became one of the most popular and critically acclaimed series in the world. The books follow the adventures of Harry Potter, a young wizard who attends Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, and his friends Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger. They face various challenges and enemies, such as the evil Lord Voldemort, who wants to destroy Harry and take over the wizarding world. The books have been translated into 84 languages, sold over 600 million copies, and inspired a global media franchise, including films, video games, theme parks, and merchandise.

The largest bone in the human body is the femur, which is also known as the thigh bone. It is the longest, strongest, and heaviest bone in the entire body. The femur connects to the pelvis and keeps the body upright, allowing for bipedal locomotion. The average length of the femur is about 16-19 inches, depending on an individual’s height.

There are currently eight planets in our solar system. The planets, in order from the Sun, are Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars, followed by the gas giants Jupiter and Saturn, and the ice giants Uranus and Neptune. Pluto used to be considered the ninth planet of our solar system until 2006 when it was reclassified as a dwarf planet.

The chemical formula of water is H2O. This means that each molecule of water is made up of two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom. Water is a transparent, tasteless, odorless, and nearly colorless chemical substance that is the main constituent of Earth’s streams, lakes, and oceans, and the fluids of most living organisms.

The first artificial satellite launched by the Soviet Union was called Sputnik 1. It was launched into an elliptical low Earth orbit on October 4, 1957 as part of the Soviet space program. It was a polished metal sphere 58 cm (23 in) in diameter with four external radio antennas to broadcast radio pulses.
